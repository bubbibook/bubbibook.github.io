The Breakfast Story
Waking up was harder
then. I got used
to making her breakfast.

She followed me
out the door
and threw the bagel at me
as I biked away
Angles
Angles in the sky. Littered
building angles. How many
bikes on the road
compared to cars, like
the one that hit me today?

They said ten percent
was the chance of rain today.
It rained all day instead. But only lightly, so
how does
that count
in the greater economy of
weather
statistics?

They don’t look at me, but who looks anyways?
Do I even look at them?

Green tea man
seemed all smiles. Ginger rather
than blithe in the prior, now crass and quick
to leave the cafe stand.

My feet hurt today.
A Toothache
Red on white is a cherry
spill in spring.
She lifts her skirt
in the field
and pours the wine.

Waking up with a toothache, I noticed
one missing, a pain, and
a red stain
on the pillowcase
and she turned over.
“Casseroles to the Rescue”
Sauté bell pepper
in cast iron pan. Immortalize
your saviors.
Exalt the grey areas
of human torment. Driver slower

past houses covered in your saliva.
There is pain in the deep
green you pass by on the fells.
There’s nothing
you can do
about it. How do you start
anything anymore

when you’ve already started
so much else?

I was told to start
with what laid
before me, which is
never a good idea:

“Casseroles to the Rescue”

How have I come to
forgive you?
